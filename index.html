The sun was already halfway down, steadily sinking toward the horizon, which was barely visible behind the tall new buildings. The city itself was quite large and filled with noise: people talking and rushing along the streets, trying to catch the morning or evening bus, restless traffic capable of causing annoying traffic jams, and office centers that had become a familiar sight to the residents themselves. That evening, John didn't order anything, anticipating a dinner without delivery and extra people — he headed to his favorite local cafe. The summer rays warmed the young man's head a little, but he didn't need a hat, whether it was a panama hat or a cap. The young man didn't have a neat hairstyle, and to many it might have seemed completely unkempt (but who cares about them?), which, however, was not true: John spent a decent portion of his income on personal care, including his hair, but he simply didn't bother styling it, because it was a waste of time — and he preferred it tousled rather than slicked back or neatly combed. A pleasant bonus of the hair care products was that after the procedure, there was a pleasant apricot scent. Surprisingly, there was no oppressive heat among the wide city streets, so the guy felt comfortable in a standard-cut pastel orange cotton shirt, dark brown knee-length shorts, and his usual orange sunglasses. And thanks to the mild weather, which did not make him suffocate from the heat, the guy was able to wear sneakers that matched his shirt, complemented by high white socks. Walking along his familiar route, passing people, he made it to the café. As luck would have it, it was closed for “technical maintenance” — the sign on the door seemed to mock him silently. John rolled his eyes, mentally regretting not ordering delivery.
